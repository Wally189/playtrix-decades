What is PlayTriX?

A tiny personal operating system for your life
PlayTriX is a simple, offline “console” for your life, work and projects. It doesn’t replace your calendar, email or storage – it pulls everything together into one calm launch pad so you always know where to start.
It’s designed to feel like an operating system from another timeline: neat, opinionated, slightly retro – but focused on clarity instead of noise.

How it works

Pillars · Tiles · Links
  • Pillars are the big areas of your life (Personal, Work, Civic, Creative, Systems).
  • Tiles are shortcuts – to websites, cloud folders, documents or tools.
  • Reference is your Filofax-style page for useful info you want close by.
As you use PlayTriX, you’ll gradually build a console that feels like “home base” for your brain.

Privacy & data

No accounts. No tracking. No data collection.
PlayTriX does not send anything anywhere. There are:
  • no logins
  • no analytics
  • no cookies for tracking
  • no servers storing your information
Everything you configure is stored in your own browser’s storage on your own device. If you clear your browser data, PlayTriX forgets – which is why there’s a backup system in the Settings page so you can keep a copy.

Password protection

An extra lock on top of your usual passwords
PlayTriX includes an optional lock screen. It is not meant to replace the passcode for your phone or laptop – it simply keeps your console private if someone else is using your device.
  • You can set your own PlayTriX password.
  • You can change it or remove it at any time.
  • The password is stored only on your device, not online.
You’ll find the password options in the dedicated Password screen and in the Settings → Security area once those are turned on.

Adding your own links

Drag. Drop. Done.
Any place you can copy a link, you can turn it into a PlayTriX tile.
  • Open the site, folder or app in your browser.
  • Copy the address (URL) from the address bar.
  • Drop or paste it into a PlayTriX tools row (Console or Tools page).
This works for OneDrive folders, Google Drive files, email, WhatsApp Web, parish websites, banking, to-do apps – anything with a link.

Use it on different devices

Phones, tablets, laptops, desktops
PlayTriX is just a website, so it runs anywhere you have a modern browser: iPhone, Android, Windows, Mac, iPad, Chromebook and more.
Each device keeps its own setup. If you want the same console on several devices, you can export your PlayTriX setup as a backup file on one device and import it on another using the Settings → Backup tools section.

Make it feel like an app

Add PlayTriX to your home screen or dock
iPhone / iPad (Safari)
  • Open the PlayTriX site in Safari.
  • Tap the Share button.
  • Scroll and tap Add to Home Screen.
  • Give it a name (for example “PlayTriX”) and tap Add.
Android (Chrome / Edge / Brave)
  • Open PlayTriX in your browser.
  • Tap the ⋮ menu.
  • Choose Add to Home screen or Install app.
  • Confirm – it will now open full-screen like an app.
Windows / Mac (Chrome or Edge)
  • Open PlayTriX in Chrome or Edge.
  • In Chrome: open the ⋮ menu → Install PlayTriX….
  • In Edge: open Apps → Install this site as an app.

Connecting OneDrive & Google Drive

Cloud storage, your way
PlayTriX doesn’t try to replace cloud storage – it simply gives you fast, predictable entry points into the places you already use.
OneDrive
  • Open OneDrive in your browser.
  • Navigate to the folder or file you use most.
  • Copy the address from the address bar.
  • Create a tile in PlayTriX and paste that link in.
Google Drive
  • Open Google Drive in your browser.
  • Right-click a folder or file → Get link (make sure you can open it yourself).
  • Copy the link and paste it into a PlayTriX tile.
Over time, your console becomes a doorway into the exact files and locations that matter, without you needing to remember where everything lives.

Backups & resets

Keeping your setup safe
Because browsers can forget data when you clear history or change device, PlayTriX includes a simple backup system in Settings → Backup tools.
  • Export setup – save your console as a small file.
  • Import setup – load that file on the same or another device.
  • Copy backup string – a text version you can paste into notes.
If anything ever goes wrong, you can reset PlayTriX and restore from your own backup. No one else ever sees your data.
