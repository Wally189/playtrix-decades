Favourite browser

Choose the browser that feels like “home”
This section is purely for you. It doesn’t change your system defaults – it simply reminds you which browser you’ve chosen as your “PlayTriX home”.
Pick the one that matches how you actually work:
• Chrome – everything + extensions + Google life
• Edge – Microsoft world, OneDrive, Office, work setups
• Firefox – privacy-minded, customisable, slightly stubborn
• Safari – Apple ecosystem, iPhone/iPad/Mac harmony
• Brave – privacy and ad-blocking built in
• Opera – sidebars, extras, “why yes I am a bit different”
In a later version of PlayTriX you’ll be able to save this choice and show a matching icon on your Console. For now, treat this as a gentle nudge: pick one main browser and let everything else be “backup”.

Filofax reference

Weights & measures
Metric → Imperial (approx.)
• 1 kg ≈ 2.2 lb
• 500 g ≈ 1.1 lb (about a pound)
• 250 g ≈ 9 oz
• 1 litre ≈ 1.75 pints
• 100 ml ≈ 3.5 fl oz
Kitchen shorthand
• 1 tsp ≈ 5 ml
• 1 tbsp ≈ 15 ml
• 1 cup ≈ 240 ml (roughly a mug)
Temperatures
Quick conversions
• °F → °C : (°F − 32) ÷ 2 ≈ °C (rough enough for weather)
• °C → °F : (°C × 2) + 32 ≈ °F
Kitchen oven guide
• 160°C ≈ 325°F – slow/medium
• 180°C ≈ 350°F – standard baking
• 200°C ≈ 400°F – hot oven
Clothing sizes (rough guide)
Men’s shirts
• UK 14½–15" ≈ S
• UK 15½–16" ≈ M / L
• UK 16½–17" ≈ XL
Shoes (men)
• UK 7 ≈ EU 41
• UK 8 ≈ EU 42
• UK 9 ≈ EU 43
• UK 10 ≈ EU 44–45
Time zones
All relative to UK time (GMT / BST):
• Ireland – same as UK
• Central Europe – UK + 1 hour
• Eastern Europe – UK + 2 hours
• US Eastern – UK − 5 hours (approx.)
• US Pacific – UK − 8 hours (approx.)
• India – UK + 5½ hours
• Australia (Sydney) – UK + 9 to +11 hours (seasonal)
Paper sizes
A-series
• A4 – 210 × 297 mm (everyday sheets)
• A5 – half of A4
• A3 – double A4
• A6 – postcard-ish
Other
• US Letter – 8.5" × 11"
• US Legal – 8.5" × 14"
Symbols & numerals
Common currency symbols
• £ – pound sterling
• € – euro
• $ – dollar
• ¥ – yen / yuan
Roman numerals
• I = 1 · V = 5 · X = 10
• L = 50 · C = 100 · D = 500 · M = 1000
Example: MCMLXXV = 1975
Odds & ends
A few tiny facts that are handy when your brain is tired:
• 52 weeks in a year · 365 days (366 leap years)
• 10,000 steps ≈ 7–8 km for most people
• 1 GB ≈ 1000 MB (ish – tech people will argue, your Filofax does not care)
• Rule of thumb: emergencies – dial 112 or 999 in the UK & EU

Home & memory

Recipes & meal ideas
Keep your recipes wherever feels natural, then point PlayTriX at them.
Ideas:
• A Word document in OneDrive called “Family Recipes”
• A OneNote section just for recipes
• A Google Doc or Google Keep list
• A Notion database with tags (quick, cheap, freezer-friendly…)
Then add a tile on the Tools page called Recipes and paste the link in. One click, and you’re in your recipe book.
Photo albums
Same pattern for photos – PlayTriX is the doorway, your cloud is the cupboard.
Options:
• A shared album in Google Photos
• A OneDrive “Pictures” or “Family” folder
• An iCloud shared album on Apple devices
Create a tile on the Console or Tools page called Photo album and paste the album link. Instant nostalgia button.

Quiet learning & AI helpers

Microsoft learning (SharePoint, Teams & more)
A few official places to learn the tools that many workplaces already use:
• Microsoft Learn
• Microsoft 365 training & support
• SharePoint help & learning
If you want to get quietly brilliant at SharePoint, Teams or OneDrive, these are good starting points.
Google learning
For people who live in Gmail, Drive and Docs:
• Google Workspace Learning Center
• Workspace training
A couple of hours here and there turns you into “the person who just knows how to do it”.
Other free learning resources
A few general “level up your brain” places:
• Khan Academy
• freeCodeCamp
• OpenLearn (Open University)
You can create tiles for any of these on the Tools page if you want your OS to nudge you toward learning.
My favourite AI helpers
Use AI like a smart colleague, not a replacement for your own judgement.
• ChatGPT (“Queen Bob”) – writing, planning, code, untangling messy thoughts
• Microsoft Copilot – integrated with Microsoft 365, good for work documents
• Other tools as they appear – always check who made them, what data they keep, and whether you trust that.
You can pin your preferred AI to the Console (for example: a tile called Ask Bob) so help is always one click away.
